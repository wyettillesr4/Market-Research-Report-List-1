<p><p>The whole juvenile life insurance market comprises three primary types: survival insurance, death insurance, and full insurance. Survival insurance provides coverage that pays out if the insured child survives to a certain age, promoting long-term financial planning. Death insurance offers a payout to beneficiaries in the event of the child's untimely death. Full insurance combines both aspects, providing benefits for survival and a death benefit, ensuring comprehensive financial protection for families throughout the child's life.</p></p>
