<p><p>The hydrogels for tissue engineering market is primarily categorized into natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels, derived from biological sources like collagen, alginate, and hyaluronic acid, offer excellent biocompatibility and promote cellular interactions, making them suitable for applications like wound healing and regenerative medicine. In contrast, synthetic hydrogels are engineered from polymers such as polyethylene glycol (PEG) and polyvinyl alcohol (PVA), providing customizable properties like mechanical strength and degradation rates, which allows for tailored applications in a variety of tissue engineering scenarios.</p></p>
