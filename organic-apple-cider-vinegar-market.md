<p><p>The organic apple cider vinegar market is primarily divided into two types: filtered and unfiltered. Filtered apple cider vinegar undergoes a processing method that removes impurities and sediments, resulting in a clear liquid. This type is often favored for its smoother taste and extended shelf life. In contrast, unfiltered apple cider vinegar retains the "mother," a collection of beneficial bacteria and enzymes, providing a cloudy appearance. Many consumers prefer unfiltered for its potential health benefits and natural qualities.</p></p>
