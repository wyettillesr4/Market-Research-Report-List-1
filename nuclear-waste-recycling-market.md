<p><p>The nuclear waste recycling market encompasses various disposal methods. Direct disposal involves burying waste in deep geological formations, isolating it from the environment. Underwater storage utilizes submerged containers, leveraging water’s shielding properties to contain radiation. Nuclear waste vitrification transforms waste into glass, immobilizing harmful substances for safer long-term storage. Other methods may include advanced technologies like pyroprocessing or transmutation, aiming to minimize waste volume and toxicity. Each approach seeks to balance safety, efficiency, and environmental impact.</p></p>
