<p><ul><li>Linear</li><li>Switched</li><li>Battery-based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Power supplies for telecom systems can be classified into three main types: linear, switched, and battery-based. Linear power supplies provide a stable voltage with low noise, making them ideal for sensitive equipment but are less efficient and bulkier. Switched power supplies are lightweight and efficient, utilizing high-frequency switching to convert power but may introduce electromagnetic interference. Battery-based systems offer backup power, ensuring reliability during outages, and can support remote locations where grid power is unavailable. Each type serves unique needs in telecom applications.</p></p>
